Alt-Right: Age of Rage: Extremism in America

A Summary of Documentary by Lough

In the tumultuous first year of Donald Trump’s presidency, filmmaker Adam Bhala Lough delved into the heart of a growing movement that would come to be known as the “alt-right.” His documentary, “Alt-Right: Age of Rage,” provides a raw and unflinching look at the ideological components, leaders, and tragic events that unfolded during this period.

The Rise of the Alt-Right

The term “alt-right” encompasses a political grouping that combines elements of racism, white nationalism, anti-Semitism, and populism. It has been embraced by white supremacists and nationalist groups who emphasize the preservation and protection of the white race in the United States. But what fuels this movement, and how did it gain momentum?

The Charlottesville Tragedy

The documentary centers around the “Unite the Right” rally held in Charlottesville, Virginia in August 2017. Organized by the alternative right, this rally aimed to show support for preserving Confederate monuments and memorials. However, the event turned violent when white nationalists clashed with counter-demonstrators, many of whom identified as Antifa (anti-fascists).

The violence culminated in the tragic death of Heather Heyer, a counter-protester who was run down by a car driven by a neo-Nazi supporter. The aftermath of this event shook the nation and highlighted the deep divisions within American society.

Ideological Roots and Anger

Lough explores the factors that fueled the alt-right movement:


White Identity: The ideology of the alt-right cannot be separated from discussions about white identity. Many followers believe that white people are being marginalized and pushed aside by people of color, immigrants, Muslims, and even women. They long for a return to a perceived golden era when traditional gender roles prevailed.
Rejection of Mainstream Conservatism: The alt-right rejects mainstream Republicanism, considering it a betrayal of their cause. They view mainstream Republicans as having sold out their party.

Social Media and Emboldenment

Lough also sheds light on how white nationalist social media platforms bolstered the alt-right movement. Additionally, President Trump’s controversial comments following the Charlottesville violence—where he placed blame “on many sides”—emboldened the movement further.

A Snapshot of Violent Racism

“Alt-Right: Age of Rage” captures a pivotal moment in American history. The images from the rally, including Heather Heyer’s tragic death, serve as a stark reminder of the cyclical grip of violent racism.

As we grapple with the legacy of this movement, understanding its roots and motivations becomes crucial. Lough’s documentary invites viewers to confront uncomfortable truths and engage in meaningful dialogue about the future of our nation.

Release Date: March 9, 2018 (United States)
